International Press Corps

IPJ

Journalism plays a crucial role in any Model United Nations. While delegates debate, negotiate, and pass resolutions, it is the journalists who ensure that discussions are transparent, accessible, and impactful. In an MUN, the Journalism Committee mirrors the real-world press, reporting on proceedings, highlighting key arguments, and questioning delegates on their policies and strategies.

Journalists are not passive observers; they actively shape the narrative of the conference. Through articles, press releases, interviews, and even breaking news updates, they hold delegates accountable and bring out perspectives that may otherwise go unnoticed. Their work ensures that the committee floor remains dynamic, as delegates must always be prepared to justify their actions in the public eye.

Moreover, journalism adds creativity and engagement to MUNs. It provides a space for witty headlines, sharp analysis, and sometimes even satire, making the conference more vibrant. For participants, it is an opportunity to develop writing, interviewing, and analytical skills that are as important as debating.

In short, without journalism, an MUN would lose its voice. It is the press that connects the committees, captures the essence of debate, and tells the story of the conference.

IPP

Photography is one of the most crucial yet often underappreciated aspects of any Model United Nations. While debates, negotiations, and resolutions form the core of the conference, it is photography that captures these moments and turns them into lasting memories. A well-timed photograph can tell the story of a heated debate, the intensity of a negotiation, or the joy of a delegate’s victory far better than words alone.

In MUNs, photographers act as silent storytellers. They move across committees, documenting not just the proceedings but also the emotions, the teamwork, and the camaraderie that make the conference special. From formal shots of delegates in action to candid captures of laughter and behind-the-scenes moments, photography adds depth and life to the event.

Beyond memories, photography also plays a professional role. The images are used for reports, media coverage, and promotions, showcasing the success of the conference to a wider audience. They highlight the dedication of organizers and the vibrancy of the participants.

In essence, photography ensures that while debates may end, the spirit of the MUN lives on — preserved in frames that can be cherished forever.
